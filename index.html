Introduction

Road accidents have become very common these days. Looking at the numbers, it seems like a national concern. Approximately 1.25 million people die in road crashes each year. 3287 deaths occur each day on an average. Around 20-50 people are injured or disabled anually. Despite of the stringent traffic guidelines and regulations, Road accidents is one of the most common reasons for deaths, in fact it ranks as the 9th leading cause of death and accounts for 2.2% of all deaths globally. In USA, over 37000 people die in road crashed each year and 2.35 million are injured or disabled. Road crashes costs US $230.6 billion per year, on an average $820 per person. Road crashes are the single greatest annual cause of death of healthy U.S. citizens travelling abroad. Source - https://www.asirt.org/safe-travel/road-safety-facts/

These facts and numbers are indeed very alarming and disturbing.

This is a countrywide traffic accident dataset, which covers 49 states of the United States. We will just analyze the data for 31st December 2020. 31st december is probably the busiest day in Christmas every year. We will analyse the data and uncover some usefull insights related to the accidents that happened on 31st December 2020.

Overall Severity of the accidents across different states in the USA.

In the below visualization, the latitudes and longitudes of where the accidents occured are plotted on the US map boundaries. In simple terms, this means the exact location of where the accidents happened is plotted on the US map. This visualization will help us understand what states/region within the US were most accident prones. There is also an option of selecting different map projections for the plot. You can select your different projects for the map here:

Just as we predicted, this visualization helped us uncover some of the most accident prone states. Looking at the maps, it seems like California tops the list. We can also see that apart from California, Florida, South Carolina, Wisconsin and Oregon were some of the states where most of the accidents occured. It is alarming but very obvious to see some of the most busiest cities like New york, New Jersey, Philadelphia, Washington DC and Boston having dense blue circles which clearly demonstrates that a lot of accidents happened there.

Histograms for: Temperature/Wind Chill/ Humidity/ Wind Speed.

This visualization gives an overall picture of different conditions (Temperature, Wind Chill, Humidity and Wind speed) that were recorded when the accidents happened. These are some of the most important factors that might provide us a lot of information about the accidents. This information that we will uncover here, might help us in avoiding accidents in the future.

Please select one parameter from the list:

Temperature: Looking at the graph, we can say that most of the accidents have occured when the temperature was a bit cool. We can see some tall buildings between (35F - 50F or 2C - 10C). Wind Chill: Wind Chill also has a similar trend. It is infered since the temperature was cooler, the wind might have been cooler too. Humidity: We can see that the more humid the climate was, more accidents have tend to have happened. We can see some tall buildings between 75% - 100%. Wind Speed: Surprisingly, this graph tells us that most of the accidents happened when the wind was stable. That means that wind speed had negligible impact on occurance of these accidents.

Number of accidents occured across individual states.

We already saw the accidents across different states in the Map plot above which gave us an overall picture about which were the accident prone states. Below graph will help us in finding out which were the most accidents prone states.

As we can see, California, Florida, Texas, Oregon and Minnesota are out top 10 accident prone states.

A Histogram of Weather Conditions

Below is a graph of weather conditions that were recorded when the accidents took place. Lets see under what weather conditions most of the accidents happened.

Most of the accidents happened when the weather was cloudy. That makes sense as there must have been low visibility. There were also lot of accidents occuring when the climate was clear (fair). Some of the accidents happened when the climate was Mostly cloudy and partly cloudy.

Seems like weather moderately affected the accidents that took place.
